Bhindi (Okra) Stir-fry

Ingredients

  • 250g Bhindi (Okra), cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 1 tbsp oil
  • A pinch of Hing (Asafoetida)
  • ½ tsp Ajwain (Carom seeds)
  • ½ tsp Red Chili Powder
  • ½ tsp Salt (adjust to taste)
  • 1 tsp Dhaniya Powder (Coriander powder)
  • ¼ tsp Garam Masala (add at the end)
  • ½ tsp Amchur (Dry Mango Powder, to avoid stickiness)

Instructions

  • Wash and dry the Bhindi thoroughly. Cut them into 1-inch pieces, removing the ends. Dry the Bhindi completely with a towel to avoid sliminess while cooking.
  • In a pan, heat 1 tablespoon of oil on medium heat. Once the oil is hot, add a pinch of Hing and ½ teaspoon of Ajwain. Let them sizzle for a few seconds.
  • Add ½ teaspoon of Red Chili Powder and stir quickly to avoid burning the spices.
  • Add the cut Bhindi to the pan and sauté on medium-high heat. Stir the Bhindi occasionally to prevent it from sticking to the pan.
  • After a few minutes, add ½ teaspoon of salt and 1 teaspoon of Dhaniya powder. Stir well, ensuring the Bhindi is evenly coated with the spices.
  • Add ½ teaspoon of Amchur powder to reduce any sliminess. Stir and cook the Bhindi for another 5-7 minutes until it becomes soft and slightly crisp.
  • Once the Bhindi is cooked, sprinkle ¼ teaspoon of Garam Masala and stir it.
