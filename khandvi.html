Khandvi

Ingredients

  • ½ cup Besan (Gram Flour)
  • ½ cup Dahi (Curd)
  • 1 cup Water
  • A pinch of Haldi (Turmeric Powder)
  • Salt (to taste)
  • ½ tsp Green Chili Paste (adjust as per taste)
  • ½ tsp Lemon Juice
  • For Tadka (Tempering):
  • 1 tbsp Oil
  • ½ tsp Rai (Mustard Seeds)
  • 6-7 Curry Leaves

Instructions

  • In a bowl, whisk together besan and dahi until smooth. Then add water, haldi, salt, and green chili paste. Mix well to form a lump-free batter.
  • Add lemon juice and stir again.
  • Pour the mixture into a pan and cook on low-medium heat, stirring continuously until it thickens and forms a smooth paste.
  • To check if it’s ready, spread a little batter on a greased plate; if it rolls easily after cooling, it's done.
  • Quickly spread the thickened batter evenly on a greased plate or surface.
  • Sprinkle grated coconut on top and let it cool slightly.
  • Cut into strips and gently roll them into Khandvi rolls.
  • Tempering:
  • Heat 1 tbsp oil in a pan. Add mustard seeds and curry leaves, letting them splutter.
  • Pour this tempering over the Khandvi rolls for added flavor.
